# Glossary

The terms used across this repo, plus what they actually mean.

---

### Orchestrator

The highest level of AI fluency. Not a user. Not an operator. A team lead who directs multiple AI agents — each with a specialty — toward a deliverable. The orchestrator sets the brief, loads the context, defines the constraints, and verifies the work. The tools don't play until the orchestrator points at them.

**Why it matters:** "Orchestrator" is the role this repo argues every AI-fluent student should grow into. It's also a real and emerging job description. People at Anthropic, Deloitte, and a hundred other firms are doing this work full-time today.

---

### The Three Levers

ASK, CONTEXT, CONSTRAINTS. The minimum-viable framework for prompting. Every other framework you've seen — CRAFT, RACE, CO-STAR, OCTAVE — is repackaging these three. See [`01-the-levers/README.md`](./01-the-levers/README.md).

---

### Trust and Glue (Clark & Chalmers, 1998)

A philosophical framework for when a tool stops being a tool and becomes part of your thinking. Three criteria: **Reliability** (it's there when you need it), **Trustworthiness** (you endorse its output the way you endorse your own memory), **Accessibility** (you can reach it easily).

**Why it matters:** This was written for *passive* tools — notebooks, address books, smartphones. This repo argues it's incomplete for *active* AI, which led to the fourth pillar.

---

### Maintainability (the 4th Pillar)

The fourth pillar Trust and Glue is missing in the AI era. The permanent right — and the technical ability — to evaluate the system, audit it, and turn it off. Your kill switch.

**Why it matters:** Without Maintainability, you become an employee of the tool. With it, you stay the team lead.

---

### Fluency Trap

The cognitive bias that smooth, articulate AI text feels true even when it's wrong. The brain treats easy-to-read as TRUE. The smoother the AI sounds, the harder it is to catch a hallucination.

**Why it matters:** This is the #1 reason students get hurt by AI. The fix is generative friction (below).

---

### Generative Friction

The opposite of the fluency trap. Every time the AI pushes back, contradicts you, or outright hallucinates, that's friction — and friction is the gift. It jolts you out of passivity and forces you to engage. **Don't chase frictionless AI. Chase friction that makes you sharper.**

---

### Sparring Partner (vs. Yes-Man)

A mindset for AI conversations. A yes-man agrees with you and gives you what you asked for. A sparring partner makes you defend your thinking. The best AI interaction isn't when it agrees with you — it's when it makes you DEFEND your reasoning.

---

### CRAFT

A prompting acronym you'll see online: Context, Role, Action, Format, Tone. Same three muscles as ASK / CONTEXT / CONSTRAINTS, just sliced differently. Don't memorize. CRAFT is one example among many — RACE, CO-STAR, OCTAVE, BANANA — and the point is that the *acronym* doesn't matter; the *discipline* does.

---

### Skills (Anthropic)

A claude.ai feature that bundles a reusable prompt + instructions + sometimes example outputs into a one-click installable component. Once installed, you invoke it like a command. The two Skills shipped in [`03-claude-skills/`](./03-claude-skills/) of this repo are examples.

**Why it matters:** Skills are how you stop re-typing the same prompt every time. Build it once, run it forever.

---

### Prompter → Copilot → Agent → Orchestrator

The four-stage progression of AI fluency this repo uses to map where you are.

- **Prompter** — copy, paste, pray. Reactive. Most people start here.
- **Copilot** — you've learned some tricks. Specific asks. Better outputs. The middle of the spectrum.
- **Agent** — you've started using AI to take *actions* (file changes, calendar invites, code commits), not just produce text.
- **Orchestrator** — you direct multiple AI agents like a team lead. Set the deliverable, brief the team, verify the work.

The journey is asymmetric: most people stall at Copilot. The jump to Orchestrator is a discipline change, not a skill change.

---

### Extracted Mind vs. Extended Mind

Two paths the discipline describes.

- **Extracted Mind** — AI does the thinking *for* you. You skip the structure. Skill erodes. Judgment fades. The "Google Maps into the lake" failure mode.
- **Extended Mind** — AI thinks *with* you. You bring the structure, AI amplifies your reasoning. You remain the expert.

Every time you open ChatGPT, you're choosing which path.

---

### Brief the Bot

The repo's tagline and the working metaphor for the entire discipline. The premise: AI is your team. You're the team lead. You don't *use* the AI; you *brief* it — like you'd brief a junior analyst on day one.

If the bullet point isn't a brief, it's a wish. Wishes don't ship.
